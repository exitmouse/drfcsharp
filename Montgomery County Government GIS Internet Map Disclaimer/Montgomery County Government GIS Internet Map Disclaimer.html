Montgomery County Government DTS-GIS Internet Map Disclaimer


The Montgomery County Department of Technology Services Geographic Information Systems (DTS-GIS) Internet Map services were developed to facilitate public access of information with regard to elections, public safety, public works, transit services, and street addresses, etc.. The data presented in the DTS-GIS Internet Map Services, including the Map Room on the Net, the Digital Aerial Photo Image Server, and the Route Mapper map server, are believed to be accurate. However, the Montgomery County Government and its data suppliers do not guarantee and make no warranties for the accuracy of the data presented in the DTS-GIS Internet Map Services. Consequently, in no event shall Montgomery County Government become liable to DTS-GIS Internet Map Server users, or any other party, for any loss or damages, consequential or otherwise, including but not limited to time, money, or goodwill, arising from the use, operation or modification of the data presented. Therefore, by clicking any DTS-GIS Internet Map Service hyperlinks, users agree to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Montgomery County Government for any and all liability of any nature arising out of or resulting from the lack of accuracy or correctness of the data, or the use of the data presented in the DTS-GIS Internet Map Services.

Special Note: Property data layer (if present) is a combination of data from Montgomery County Park and Planning Commission (P&P), Maryland State Department of Assessment & Taxation (SDAT), and the cities of Rockville and Gaithersburg. The map graphics was created by tracing the traditional paper tax maps and force fitting the digitized line work onto planimetric basemaps (edges of pavement, building footprints, etc.) As such, it is only an approxmiate representation of the land ownership parcels. It is definitely not a legal land record. Attributes associated with property parcels were extracted from SDAT database. Linkages between property parcels and attributes were originally created by DTS-GIS and are being maintained by P&P and the cities. DTS-GIS assembles the property data layer from the four source agencies quarterly. We are encouraged by a positive feedback from a web user.
